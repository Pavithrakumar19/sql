OBJECTIVE

The purpose of this analysis is to derive actionable business insights from the student enrollment and performance data using SQL. These insights can help academic management improve course offerings, teaching quality, and student outcomes.

[Enrollment Insights]
1️.Department-wise Student Distribution

-Computer Science and Data Science departments have the highest student enrollment.

-Electronics and Mechanical departments have comparatively lower enrollment, indicating potential scope for promotion or curriculum enhancement.

Business Impact:

Allocate more faculty and resources to high-enrollment departments.

Introduce awareness programs for low-enrollment departments.

2️.Course Demand Analysis

-Web Development and Machine Learning courses show high enrollment frequency.

-Specialized courses like Manufacturing Processes have lower participation.

Business Impact:

Expand popular courses with additional batches.

Review curriculum relevance of low-demand courses.

[Performance Insights]
3️. Top Performing Students

-Multiple students maintain an average score above 85.

-High performers are mostly from Computer Science and Data Science.

Business Impact:

Introduce merit-based scholarships.

Encourage top students for mentoring or research roles.

4️. Department-wise Academic Performance

-Data Science department has the highest average marks.

Electronics department shows below-average performance.

Business Impact:

-Share best teaching practices across departments.

-Provide academic support or curriculum revision where needed.

5️.Courses Requiring Attention

-Courses with average marks below 75 indicate learning gaps.

-Business Impact:

-Improve course materials.

-Conduct remedial sessions or faculty training.
